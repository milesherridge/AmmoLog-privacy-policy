AmmoLog – Privacy Policy

Effective date: 6 April 2025  ·  Last updated: 6 April 2025

AmmoLog is a shooting session logger. All data you enter — firearms, ammunition, and session records — is stored locally on your device and is never transmitted to us or any third party, with the single exception of anonymous crash reports described below.

1. Who we are

AmmoLog is an Android application developed and published by M Herridge. If you have any privacy questions, contact us at ammolog.app@gmail.com.

2. Data you provide

The app allows you to record:

  • Firearm names and calibres
  • Ammunition types and calibres
  • Shooting session dates and round counts

This data is stored exclusively in an encrypted SQLite database on your device. It is not uploaded, synced, or shared with us or any third party. You are in full control: you can export or delete this data at any time from within the app.

3. Crash reporting – Firebase Crashlytics

AmmoLog uses Firebase Crashlytics, a crash-reporting service provided by Google LLC, to help us identify and fix bugs. When the app crashes or encounters an error, Crashlytics automatically collects and sends a report containing:

  • Device model, manufacturer, and Android version
  • App version and build number
  • A stack trace describing where the crash occurred
  • Time and date of the crash
  • A randomly generated Crashlytics installation identifier

This data does not include your name, email address, or any firearm or ammunition records you have entered. The installation identifier cannot be linked back to you personally.

Crash reports are processed by Google on our behalf and are subject to Google's privacy policy: policies.google.com/privacy. For more information about Crashlytics specifically, see firebase.google.com/support/privacy.

4. Data we do not collect

We do not collect, store, or process:

  • Your name, email address, or account information — the app requires no account
  • Your location or GPS data
  • Your contacts, camera, or microphone data
  • Any firearm or ammunition records you enter
  • Advertising identifiers or browsing history

5. Backups

If you use the in-app backup feature, a backup file is saved to a location of your choosing on your own device or connected storage. We do not have access to this file. Android system backup is disabled for the app, so your data is not automatically backed up to Google's servers via the Android backup service.

6. Exports

The export feature generates a file (CSV or PDF) from your records and places it in a location you choose. We do not receive a copy of exported files.

7. Children

AmmoLog is not directed at children under 13. We do not knowingly collect any information from children.

8. Changes to this policy

If we make material changes to this policy, we will update the effective date above and, where appropriate, notify users via an in-app notice. Continued use of the app after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
