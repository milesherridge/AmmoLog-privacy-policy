AmmoLog Privacy Policy

Last updated: April 2026

AmmoLog is a shooting session log app for Android. This policy explains what data the app collects and how it is handled.

No data is collected

AmmoLog does not collect, transmit, share, or sell any personal data or usage information. The app has no internet connection, no analytics, no advertising, and no third-party SDKs that collect data.

What is stored on your device

Everything you enter into AmmoLog — firearm names, serial numbers, ammunition types, session dates, round counts, and notes — is stored locally on your device only. This data never leaves your device unless you explicitly export or back it up yourself using the app's built-in export and backup features.

Encryption

All data is stored in an encrypted database on your device using AES-256 encryption. The encryption key is stored in your device's secure hardware keystore and is unique to your device.

Backups and exports

When you use the Backup or Export features, AmmoLog creates a file and hands it to Android's standard file-sharing system. You choose where that file goes. AmmoLog has no visibility into or control over files once they leave the app.

Permissions

AmmoLog requests no permissions. It does not access your camera, location, contacts, microphone, or any other device resource.

Children

AmmoLog is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect any information from anyone.
